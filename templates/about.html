Graffiti on your phone

A collaborative, creative "graPhotty experience". Take an artistic sketchy snap; draw on our window; turn and shake your phone for a wintery snow storm.

About

GraPHOtti is a collaborative, digital Graffiti experience, designed for WestEndLights, which is one night in the year when the front windows of the houses turn into screens and light up the streets with short films, shadows and light to surprise, delight and enchant local residents and passers-by. It next takes place in the Westend of Lincoln, UK, on Friday, 6 December 2019.

GraPHOtti allows you to post sketch photos and draw on the front window of 10 Queens Crescent with your smartphone. Just go to https://lcas.lincoln.ac.uk/graphotti/ to snap and draw away (or scan the QR code or tap your NFC-enabled phone on the tag in front of the house). As this is a community experience, you need to be close to Lincoln's Westend to be able to draw. Its development began in 2015, and year on year, some new features have been added. Explore again this year.

Come to our Window to snap and draw away with others, share the current Window content on Twitter, follow what others are sharing.

New Features

New in 2019:

Snap a sketchy background photo to draw on with your own phone.

New in 2018:

Shake your phone, and witness a snow storm wiping away your drawing. Turn your phone to turn the "snow globe".
